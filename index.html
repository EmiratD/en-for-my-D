present simple.

Use:

  • facts
  • constant actions
  • schedules

Marker-words:

  • always
  • never
  • every day(week, month, year)
  • seldom
  • usually
  • regularly
  • rarely
  • sometimes

To be

affirmative:

S + am/is/are + predicate...

example:

  1. i am a student.
  2. we are ready.
  3. she is one of them.
  4. they are red.

negative:

S + am/is/are + not + predicate...

example:

  1. I am not a student.
  2. we are not ready.
  3. she is not one of them
  4. they are not red.

question:

am/is/are + S + predicate?...

example:

  1. am I a student?
  2. are not we ready?
  3. is not she one of them?
  4. are not they red?

Verbs

affirmative:

S + V/Vs + object...

example:

  1. you come home every day.
  2. We live in the city.
  3. she drinks cup of coffee every day.
  4. they check homework every day.

negative:

S + don't/doesn't + V + object...

exemple:

  1. Usually you don't come home at night.
  2. he doesn't live in the city.
  3. she doesn't drink cup of coffee every day.
  4. they don't check homework every day.

question:

do/does + S + V + object...?

exemple:

  1. do you come home at night everday?
  2. does he live in the city?
  3. does she drink cup of coffee everyday?
  4. do they check homework everyday?

present continuous.

Use:

  • Thing that are happening now
  • Temporary events
  • A new pattern or habit
  • Future plans

Marker-words:

  • currently
  • today
  • tonight
  • now
  • at present
  • at this moment/at the moment
  • still

To be

affirmative:

S + am/is/are + being + predicate...

exemple:

  1. she is being quiet.
  2. I am being so cold.
  3. he is being one of them.
  4. they are being rebel.

negative:

S + am/is/are + not + being + predicate...

exemple:

  1. she is not being quiet.
  2. I am not being so cold.
  3. he is not being one of them.
  4. they are not being rebel.

question:

am/is/are + S + being + predicate?...

exemple:

  1. is she being quiet?
  2. am I being so cold?
  3. is he being one of them?
  4. are they being rebel?

exemple:

am/is/are + S + Ving?
